Introduction

In this module you will learn how to compute the position of Earth orbiting
satellites using two-line orbital elements available on the Internet; how to do visibility computations for the satellites; how to compile a link budget; and how to visualize this information in various ways and from different perspectives.

Learning objectives/outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Explain the basics of orbital mechanics, Kepler elements and the effect of the Earth's gravity field on satellite orbits
  • Compute the position and velocity of satellites using two-line (Kepler) elements
  • Predict and visualize satellite visibility, look angles and transit times
  • Design various orbits types (Repeat, Sun-synchronous and Geostationary orbits).

Main tasks and deadlines

Basic concepts will be introduced in a series of short lectures and hands-on exercises in Matlab. The exercises are discussed during the class room sessions and their solution can also be used as building blocks for the assignment.
